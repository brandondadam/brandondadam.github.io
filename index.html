Since joining the VisionLink® design team at Cat®, I’ve helped lead a full platform rewrite and redesign—transforming a fragmented set of legacy tools into a single, unified experience. This consolidation enabled Cat® to retire multiple standalone apps and gave customers one centralized platform to manage their entire fleet in.

Following the platform redesign, I led the introduction of key features including Work Order Management, Fleet Planning, and Fleet Financials. This work required deep collaboration with cross-functional teams, stakeholders across Cat®’s organization, and extensive customer research through field visits. At times, progress was challenged by overlapping feedback and conflicting priorities—what often felt like “too many cooks in the kitchen.” To move forward, I helped facilitate alignment by creating space for shared understanding and leading structured workshops that prioritized customer needs while balancing internal input.

When traditional UX and Agile processes proved too rigid for the complexity and scale of this work, I initiated and led targeted design sprint workshops. These sessions helped realign cross-functional teams, streamline decision-making, and integrate diverse insights—from field research to stakeholder feedback. By grounding our direction in real customer needs, we were able to cut through noise, reduce rework, and ensure the features we delivered were both strategically impactful and highly usable.

VisionLink is on the verge of large-scale growth, with active customers increasing 10x, since the platform rewrite. The tool has helped reduce fleet downtime, improve asset utilization, and streamline health management. With new features like Work Order Management, Fleet Planning, and Asset Financials—plus deeper integration with parts, rental, and dealer services—VisionLink is evolving from a basic telematics tool into a full-scale fleet and operations management solution that stands out in the market.

About Me

I’m a digital designer with over 10 years of experience in tech; blending craft, strategy, and curiosity to create meaningful, lasting work. Over the years, I’ve grown into a design leadership role, with experience guiding teams and large-scale initiatives across both startups and enterprise environments.

Early in my career, I worked in R&D labs, consultancies, and startups. I thrived on the energy and fast pace, but over time grew frustrated with the tech industry’s fixation on speed for the sake of capturing attention, and adding features that bloated products instead of providing lasting value. That shift in perspective led me to look beyond traditional tech for career growth. For the past three years at Cat®, I’ve been designing tools that help customers step away from their desks and into the environments where their work truly happens, tackling real-world challenges in business, construction, mining, and environmental impact and equipment management. This work has allowed me to take a different approach to software, one that keeps people off their devices and respects their time and attention.

Additionally, I’ve been building KIC - a design lab reimagining what technology could be if it prioritized lasting value, ethical thinking, and curiosity over constant output, instant gratification, and mass consumerism. My goal is to challenge how we approach building and using technology, questioning assumptions and exploring alternatives that better serve people, communities, and our environment.
